Moral Psychology

The science of morality.

Moral Reasoning

Do we arrive at our sense of what's morally right and wrong through reasoning? I built this website to showcase a multi-lab research project dedicated to tackling this question.

Moral responsibility

When we accidentally hurt someone, why do we judge ourselves more harshly than other people are willing to judge us? I led an fMRI study providing neural evidence that, relative to a mere witness to the accident, we may empathize more with the victims of our own accidents.

Moral Imagination

Our willingness to help others in need is influenced by how vividly we can imagine scenes of helping. I co-authored an fMRI study where we found that neural activity in 'episodic simulation' regions of the brain can predict how likely someone is to report wanting to help a person in need.
